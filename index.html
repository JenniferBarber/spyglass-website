Approach

The work we do, defined clearly.

Spyglass is brought in when the strategy makes sense on paper, the capital is committed, and execution is the missing piece. We work directly with leadership and teams to design the operating structure that lets the plan land in the real world, then stay embedded to drive it.

The work lives in unfamiliar markets, novel technology, and politically sensitive or regulatory environments where the playbook is unwritten, ambiguous, or in motion.

An inflection point is when the opportunity is real, but the current operating model will not get you there. Post-deal, post-raise, pilot-to-production, market entry into unfamiliar terrain. The moment the existing way of working stops being enough.
Problems we solve
The opportunity is real, but not yet operational. Capital is committed, technology proven, market identified. No executable path yet exists.
Growth is the objective, but the terrain is unfamiliar. No playbook yet applies. Political, regulatory, institutional, cultural, or cross-border dynamics derail the standard approach.
Execution is underway, but strategy must evolve in motion. Assumptions break. Constraints surface. Plans must adapt without losing momentum.
Outcomes we deliver
An executable path forward. Clear priorities, sequencing, decision logic. Concept to action without unrealistic assumptions.
Credible expansion into unfamiliar markets. Growth that holds up under regulatory, institutional, and operational realities.
Adaptive momentum as conditions shift. The agile phase between concept and running it on rails. Plans evolve in motion. Teams stay decisive, not stalled.
Services

Three core engagements. Two specialized packages.

Three core engagements and two specialized packages, each scoped to a real situation with a defined deliverable and timeline. Spyglass prices on outcome, not on hours. The price is set with you against the actual scope, in writing, after a short conversation.

Inflection Point Diagnostic
2–3 weeks · Fixed price, set at scope

A focused engagement that names the real bottleneck and produces a decision-ready executable plan. Filters serious situations from tire-kickers and converts at high rates into deeper engagements when the fit is right.

Deliverables:

  • Diagnosis of the binding constraint (decision rights, capacity, incentives, friction)
  • Decision logic and sequencing
  • Stakeholder and power map
  • 90-day execution plan with named owners
  • Recommendation: handoff or embed
Transformation Sprint
30–60 days · Fixed price, set at scope

A focused, time-boxed engagement that restructures the execution pathway so the work can actually run. Removes immediate blockers, aligns the team on priorities and decision rights, and ships something real that demonstrates momentum.

Best fit: Companies with stalled transformations. Post-acquisition integration chaos. International expansion stuck on execution. Post-raise growth-stage scaleups facing their first regulated-market entry.

Embedded Transformation Lead
6–12 months · Fixed price, set at scope

A time-bound operating role. Spyglass stays involved through execution until the operating model stabilizes, then transitions out to an internal team. Outcomes by month. Not activities by week.

Best fit: PE portfolio companies in post-deal transformation. Corporate clients in international expansion. Well-funded growth companies that need a senior operator at executive level without the headcount commitment.

AI Deployment
Diagnostic
2–3 weeks · Fixed price, set at scope

For PE portfolio companies, growth-stage operators, and divisional leaders inside larger organizations deploying AI into regulated, institutional, or operationally complex environments. Most often the buyer is the person with the discretionary budget and the real problem, not a giant-firm appetite. Roughly 86 percent of enterprise AI deployments fail, and most failures are organizational, not technical. The binding constraint is rarely the technology. It is decision rights, capability uptake, and incentive alignment. The operating layer.

Includes: Use-case prioritization, capability assessment, deployment readiness audit, recommended sequencing, and a 90-day implementation roadmap.

Principal case · Cross-border biotech commercialization

Post-Capital, Pre-Operational

A first-of-kind biotech platform had real science, committed capital, and no clear path from R&D to a deployed commercial facility. Embedded as operating lead, the work spanned a Mexico greenfield build under JV terms that preserved operational and IP control, first-of-kind regulatory approvals across three jurisdictions, and a byproduct certification pathway that produced roughly 15x value uplift. The company closed a $60M round during the COVID liquidity freeze. The unlock was not more activity; it was naming what actually had to happen and aligning the organization around it.
